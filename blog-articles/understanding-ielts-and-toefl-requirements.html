Understanding IELTS and TOEFL Requirements

May 18, 2025

Students who want to study internationally often need to demonstrate English language proficiency through widely accepted tests like IELTS and TOEFL. Below is a comprehensive overview to help you understand the key requirements and how these exams impact your university applications.

What Are IELTS and TOEFL?

IELTS (International English Language Testing System) and TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language) are standardized exams designed to measure your ability to use and understand English in academic settings. Most universities in English-speaking countries require one of these tests for admission, especially for applicants from non-English speaking nations.

  • IELTS: Accepted in the UK, Australia, Canada, and some US institutions.
  • TOEFL: Preferred by most US universities and also accepted in Canada and Australia.

IELTS Requirements

IELTS is scored on a band scale from 1 (non-user) to 9 (expert). Universities typically require overall band scores between 6.0 and 7.5 for undergraduate and postgraduate admissions, sometimes with a minimum score in each section (Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking).

  • Undergraduate programs: Minimum scores range from 6.0 to 6.5.
  • Postgraduate programs: Requirements often range from 6.5 to 7.5.
  • Individual sections: Some universities specify minimum scores for each band (e.g., no band below 6.0).

TOEFL Requirements

TOEFL is scored out of 120, with 30 points for each section: Reading, Listening, Speaking, and Writing. Most universities set minimum requirements between 80 and 100, with top-tier schools demanding higher scores.

  • Typical undergraduate requirements: 80–90.
  • Graduate programs: 90–100 or higher at leading institutions.
  • Sectional requirements: Occasionally, there are minimum scores for each section.

Key Differences Between IELTS and TOEFL

Feature IELTS TOEFL
Format Paper-based or computer-based Computer-based
Sections Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking Reading, Listening, Speaking, Writing
Scoring Band score (1-9 per section, overall average) Combined score (out of 120)
Duration ~2 hours 45 minutes ~3 hours
Accent Focus British, Australian, international varieties Mostly North American accent

How to Meet These Requirements

Check University Websites

Admission pages list exact English language test and score requirements for your intended program.

Prepare Early

Register for your chosen exam well in advance and build your language skills through practice tests and study materials.

Retake if Necessary

Most universities accept new results if you want to improve your score.

Document Submission

Send your official scores directly to universities through the test providers.

Important Tips

  • Double-check which test is preferred by your target institutions.
  • If you have studied previously in an English-medium school, ask if you can get a waiver for these tests.
  • Prepare thoroughly as these scores can impact scholarship opportunities and admissions decisions.

Conclusion

Understanding IELTS and TOEFL requirements is essential for students planning to study internationally. Begin your preparation early and aim for scores that meet or exceed the standards of your chosen universities for the best chance of admission.
